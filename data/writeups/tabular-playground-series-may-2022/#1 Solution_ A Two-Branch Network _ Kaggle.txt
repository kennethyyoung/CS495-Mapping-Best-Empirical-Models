#1 Solution: A Two-Branch Network — Tabular Playground Series - May 2022


  #1 Solution: A Two-Branch Network

Tabular Playground Series - May 2022

<https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/tabular-playground-series-may-2022>

Solution Writeup · 1st place

Our solution is a blend of some variants of the following two-branch
network:

network

How did we arrive at this solution? The introductory text of the
competition gives a hint: /… includes a number of different feature
interactions. This competition is an opportunity to explore various
methods for identifying and exploiting these feature interactions./ Few
people knew what to do with this hint. Fortunately, @sudalairajkumar
<https://www.kaggle.com/sudalairajkumar> showed us how to identify the
feature interactions with Xgbfir <https://github.com/limexp/xgbfir>. He
even produced a dataset <https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/
sudalairajkumar/tps-may22-xgboost-feature-interactions> listing all
interactions found by the tool.

On the back of an envelope, I constructed a graph of these interactions.
The graph had two connected components:

graph

The key idea then was that we needed a network which restricted feature
interactions to the connected components of the graph (other
interactions produce noise). The two-branch network implements this
idea. The left branch learns the interactions among |['f_00', 'f_01',
'f_02', 'f_03', 'f_04', 'f_05', 'f_06', 'f_19', 'f_20', 'f_21', 'f_22',
'f_23', 'f_24', 'f_25', 'f_26', 'f_28', 'f_30', 'ch7']|, the right
branch processes |['f_07', 'f_08', 'f_09', 'f_10', 'f_11', 'f_12',
'f_13', 'f_14', 'f_15', 'f_16', 'f_17', 'f_18', 'f_29', 'ch0', 'ch1',
'ch2', 'ch3', 'ch4', 'ch5', 'ch6', 'ch8', 'ch9', 'unique_characters',
'i_02_21', 'i_05_22', 'i_00_01_26']|. Except for the two-branch
architecture, our final implementation doesn't differ from the Advanced
Keras notebook <https://www.kaggle.com/code/ambrosm/tpsmay22-advanced-
keras>.

We worked on a decision tree model as well. Restricting the feature
interactions with the hyperparameter |
interaction_constraints=[features_left, features_right]| helped LightGBM
reach an lb score of 0.99778, but this was not enough to be included in
the blend.

Special thanks go to @cabaxiom <https://www.kaggle.com/cabaxiom> for
introducing the unique_characters feature, to @wti200 <https://
www.kaggle.com/wti200> for showing how to analyze feature interactions,
to @sudalairajkumar <https://www.kaggle.com/sudalairajkumar> for
publishing the Xgbfir output, and of course to @pourchot <https://
www.kaggle.com/pourchot> who with great persistence optimized our
network architecture.
