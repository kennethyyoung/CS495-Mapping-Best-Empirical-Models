Many models and many ensembles

Binary Classification with a Bank Dataset

<https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/playground-series-s5e8>

Solution Writeup · 15th place · Aug 31, 2025

fullscreen

Hill climbing

My solution is pretty standard: made many models and many ensembles. At
some point adding more models led to slightly better CV, but not public
LB scores.

Here are my best 5 types of individual models based on private LB scores:

Model 	CV 	Public LB 	Private LB
CatBoost Optuna 	0.976787 	0.97772 	0.97732
xLearn FFM 	0.976567 	0.97740 	0.97708
XGBoost Optuna 	0.976501 	0.97736 	0.97701
Keras FM 	0.976795 	0.97740 	0.97701
LAMA DenseLight 	0.975058 	0.97629 	0.97589

I used different ensemblers, LAMA DenseLight worked the best.

The main reason I write this it to call attention to 13 features created
by genetic programming - see the notebook *here* <https://
www.kaggle.com/code/tilii7/new-features-genetic-programming>. Features
are kind of nutty and it is difficult to make sense of them. Here is a
couple of simple ones:

|train['GP_07'] = np.exp(np.cos(np.exp(train['duration'] - np.exp(np.exp(np.cos(np.exp(train['pdays'])))))))
train['GP_08'] = np.cos(np.exp(train['duration'] - np.exp(train['pdays']) - np.exp(np.exp(-0.240221))))

If you add them to the raw dataset, it will increase the score by
0.0025-0.003. That doesn't hold nearly as well after other features are
generated by counting and target encoding, but it still helped the
scores by ~0.0001. If you try them, please let me know whether they work
at all for you.

In a nutshell, it works by mimicking chromosomal cross-overs in living
organisms, which is a genetic process that ensures that all the siblings
are different even though they come from the same parents. For GP, that
means creating random features, testing their fitness (a score with
regard to the target), mixing them (crossing over), and repeating this
procedure millions of times until fit features are created. It is easy
to find more information by Googling, and this is the python package I used:

https://github.com/cavalab/ellyn <https://github.com/cavalab/ellyn>
