  That was a surprise! Here is the 1st place solution....

Regression with a Tabular California Housing Dataset

<https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/playground-series-s3e1>

Solution Writeup · 1st place · Jan 12, 2023

First - thanks for the fun competition, great public solutions and
contributions! 👍

This will be a short summary but still as some maybe are interested in
what FE/models etc. included to the solution.

Didn't had much time for this competition while doing other as well but
joined the competitions for trying some new frameworks versions in this
specific dataset.

I finally picked the AutoGluon framework and its tabular predictor for
the task.
"AutoGluon-Tabular: Robust and Accurate AutoML for Structured Data" -
https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.06505 <https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.06505>

For the FE part I used a public notebook https://www.kaggle.com/code/
dmitryuarov/ps-s3e1-coordinates-key-to-victory <https://www.kaggle.com/
code/dmitryuarov/ps-s3e1-coordinates-key-to-victory> , credit to author!

The AG trained solution is an weighted ensemble of many 8 fold trained
common architectures as xgb,lgbm,catb,RF,NN etc and it also used
bootstrap aggregation and stacking(3 levels for this one).

The final local CV score was 0.5006.

That's it! 🙂
