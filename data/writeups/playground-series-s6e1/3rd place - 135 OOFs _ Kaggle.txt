Predicting Student Test Scores

<https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/playground-series-s6e1>

Solution Writeup · 3rd place · Feb 1, 2026

Congrats to all the winners and special thanks to all the people who
shared insight and notebooks for this competition: @cdeotte <https://
www.kaggle.com/cdeotte>, @include4eto <https://www.kaggle.com/
include4eto>, @siukeitin <https://www.kaggle.com/siukeitin>,
@mikhailnaumov <https://www.kaggle.com/mikhailnaumov>,
@omidbaghchehsaraei <https://www.kaggle.com/omidbaghchehsaraei>,
@mahoganybuttstrings <https://www.kaggle.com/mahoganybuttstrings>,
@yekenot <https://www.kaggle.com/yekenot>.

My solution is pretty much the same as @tilii7 <https://www.kaggle.com/
tilii7>'s solution: just add as many diverse models to an ensemble. I
didn't really bother much with FE (I was kinda too lazy to test
anything :P).


    Models

Here are my models:

XGB 	8.59994 	8.59171

CatBoost 	8.69769 	8.66484
TabM 	8.58894 	8.58800
RealMLP 	8.59069 	8.58555
LNN 	8.61888 	8.61536

My CatBoost is bad cuz I didn't really work on it as much and it ran way
too slow (maybe I set the hyperparameters wrong or something).
